Natalie McClain

Computational Neuroscientist @ Mount Sinai

I am a PhD student at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai using computational modeling of neuroimaging data to understand the neural underpinnings of drug addiction. I'm especially interested in moving beyond tightly controlled fMRI tasks toward complex stimuli like movies, to better simulate real-world environments.

Research Interests

I am a second-year neuroscience PhD student in the Neuropsychoimaging of Addiction and Related Conditions (NARC) Lab, directed by Rita Goldstein. My work in the lab uses multimodal fMRI data to investigate how the brain responds to naturalistic stimuli like movies, how those responses relate to biological factors, craving, and treatment outcomes, and how they might be leveraged for brain-based treatments. See the Research page for more on what I'm currently working on!
